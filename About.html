About Oulu River
  • Did you know that the origin of Oulu River means “flood river”? The name supposedly originates from an old Northern Ostrobothnian dialect.
  • There are the ruins of an old castle, Oulun linna, next to a popular café/bar, Tähtitornin kahvila. The café which is actually built upon the castle’s gunpowder cellar which you can visit during the opening hours of the café! The café also hosts different events during summer time. Oh and did you know, the Swedish name for Oulu, Uleåborg, actually means literally “Oulu castle”.
  • The river is 106km long and branches out to a total of six rivers: Sanginjoki, Muhosjoki, Oisavanjoki, Utosjoki, Oilinginoja, Kutujoki. Oulu River flows into the Bay of Bothnia.
  • The river used to be a very important part of tar trade, arguably it has been the most important route. At the end of the 1800’s, the tar trade started decreasing as the usage of wooden boats (that needed tarring) also decreased.
  • Before building the hydroelectric facility, Oulu River was known as one of the best places for fishing salmon. Fishing is still a very common pastime though, and even today you can fish for salmon and trout!
  • Boat landing places include Taskisenranta, Rajahauta, Raatti, Hollihaka, Laanilanranta, Värttö, Tarhurintie, Saarela, Juurusoja, Hietasaaren Vaakunakylä and Toppilansaari. In addition to this, there are private landing places for example in Hietasaari.
  • Oulu River is also known for its rapids, and rafting down the river has a long history, spanning all the way back to the 1600’s. Rafting was done both for recreational and beneficial purposes, mostly used for tar trade, timber rafting and as a way to commute from place to place. Unfortunately, a rafting accident in 1944 that cost six lives, and building the hydroelectricity facility also dealt a devastating blow to rafting. You can still visit the different rapids though! The most famous rapids being the beautiful Koitelinkoski with almost 40 000 visitors per year.
  • Turkansaari Open Air Museum is a gorgeous historical site to visit, with a lot to see and do. Did you know that it’s Finland's second-oldest open air museum? Here you can learn more about the tar trade mentioned earlier, the location is along a once vital trade route!
